How to calculate variance?

Variance is a statistical measurement that describes the spread of numbers in a dataset. To calculate variance:

1. Find the mean (average) of the numbers.

2. Subtract the mean from each number to find the deviation from the mean.

3. Square each deviation.

4. Average the squared deviations to get the variance.

What is variance?

Variance is a measure of how much values in a dataset differ from the mean. A low variance indicates that the data points tend to be close to the mean, while a high variance indicates that the data points are spread out over a larger range of values.

For example, a set of numbers with low variance might be 4, 4, 4, 4, while a set with high variance might be 1, 10, 100.

Variance in everyday life

Understanding variance can help in various fields such as:

1. Finance: Assessing risk in investments.

2. Quality control: Measuring consistency in manufacturing processes.

3. Education: Analyzing test scores to gauge student performance variability.

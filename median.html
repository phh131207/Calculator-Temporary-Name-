How to calculate a median?

Calculating a median is the process of finding the middle value of a set of numbers when they are arranged in order. To calculate a median:

1. Identify the numbers you want to find the median of.

2. Sort the numbers in ascending order.

3. If the count of numbers is odd, the median is the middle number.

4. If the count of numbers is even, the median is the average of the two middle numbers.

What is a median?

A median is a measure of central tendency that represents the middle value of a dataset when arranged in order. It provides a way to summarize the data without being influenced by outliers.

For example, for the numbers 2, 4, 6, the median is 4. For 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, the median is 3.

Medians in everyday life

Calculating medians is useful in various real-life situations. Here are a few examples:

Finding the median income of a population.

Determining the median score in a series of tests.

Analyzing the median age of a group of people.

Evaluating the median price of houses in a neighborhood.

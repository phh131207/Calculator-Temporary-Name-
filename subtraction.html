How to calculate subtraction?

Subtraction is the process of taking one number away from another to find the difference. To calculate subtraction:

1. Take the number you want to subtract from (the minuend).

2. Choose the number you want to subtract (the subtrahend).

3. Perform the subtraction: subtract the subtrahend from the minuend.

4. The result is called the difference, which represents how much remains after subtracting the subtrahend from the minuend.

What is subtraction?

Subtraction is one of the four basic arithmetic operations, and it represents the process of finding the difference between two numbers. It is essentially the process of removing one quantity from another.

For example, subtracting 5 from 12 means finding out how much is left when 5 is taken away from 12, and the answer is 7.

Subtraction in everyday life

Subtraction is a fundamental concept used in various aspects of daily life. Here are a few examples:

1. Determining how much money is left after making a purchase.

2. Calculating the remaining time after a certain period has passed.

3. Finding out how many items are left after some have been used or sold.

4. Subtracting expenses from income to find out the net amount of money available.

How to calculate exp(x)?

The function exp(x) represents \( e \) raised to the power of \( x \). To calculate exp(x):

1. Identify the value of \( x \).

2. Use the mathematical constant \( e \) (approximately 2.718) and raise it to the power of \( x \).

3. The result is the value of \( e^x \).

What is exp?

The exp function is a mathematical function that computes \( e \) raised to a given power. It is a fundamental function in mathematics, especially in calculus and complex analysis.

For example, exp(1) equals \( e \), which is approximately 2.718.

exp in everyday life

The exp function appears in various real-life situations, such as:

Modeling continuous growth processes, like population growth.

Calculating compound interest in finance.

Analyzing decay processes in physics and chemistry.

Understanding phenomena in probability and statistics.
